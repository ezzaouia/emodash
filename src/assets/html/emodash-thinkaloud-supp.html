Emodash-Supplementary-Material-Think-Aloud

Procedure: 

The usability tests were conducted under the supervision of the first author of the paper in a quiet meeting room. Before each test, the author read a prepared script to explain the objective of the session and to inform the participant about the think-aloud method. Tutors gave their permission to record and analyze tests. The first session took around 30 minutes and second session took around one hour.

Apparatus:

We conducted the tests on a MacBook Pro (13-inch, 2.6 GHz Intel Core i5, 8GB  in  memory). We provided a mouse to tutors to use. The dashboard was deployed on localhost using real data of a pack of five learning sessions that we have permission to use, analyse, and prototype with.

Script:

First author: We would like to gather feedback regarding your experience and perception of a dashboard that we have designed for tutors of SpeakPlus.

We would like you to imagine that you have conducted some learning sessions with a learner. And, we invite you to use the dashboard to explore the emotional state of your learner.

The emotions have been gathered from video-recordings of learning sessions.

Specially we would like to know what do you think as tutor about the dashboard and which part of it works or not.

While doing so, we would like you to think out loud describing your thinking, interaction with the dashboard, frustration, confusion etc.

Feedback

Tutor’s quotes (reaction)

Observation

Test with tutor 1 : 30 minutes

Confusion:

  • Balance chart

“Positive, negative, I have a marker in the center, there is no negative emotions, it's confusing, I do not know if it's emotions, I see that positive [label] and I do not know exactly what this positive, negative slider [Balance chart] is. It seems to me that she [learner] is not feeling negative emotion, it's rather surprising [because tutor was able to  see negative emotions right next in the Radial-chart]” -- Tutor1

This early version of the Balance chart aimed at conveying a discriminative emotional state of a learner: either towards positive or negative depending on the most dominant. The figure shows only green. This was confusing as a user can see that there are negative emotions, for instance, in the radial chart right next, while the balance chart has only one type of emotion (positive, green color).

We changed this rationale, to keep both positive and negative emotions.

Confusion:

  • Player slider invisible black color and without any feedback
  • Different controls to play the video: using the slide AND w/ native  buttons of the video player

“but there is a peak firmly of fear: 26% of disgust, 25% of anger, 10% of fear, can I see what happened at that moment of the interaction” -- Tutor1

"There is no mean to know the duration of where the cursor is” -- Tutor1

“I would like to navigate the video only around the peak” -- Tutor1

The player of this early version of the session view was confusing as it was hidden between the emotions. Further, there were two different ways to play the video to explore emotions: either using the slider of the timeline or the video player.

The user was confused to play the video around a specific peak. In the beginning, the user starts clicking on peaks directly but without any result. After a while, the user played the video using the play button of the video player, however, the video starts from the beginning. Besides, the tooltip did not display the time duration of the peak in the video.

We showed the slider to the tutor explaining how it can be used to navigate through the video. The tutor said: “it's not very clear [..] I did not see it” -- Tutor1

We changed the rationale by adding direct navigation using the streamgraph so that the user can click directly on peaks to explore the video.

We also changed the native video player with custom-built player to remove the confusion of having two sliders to control the video.

We added colors, grey and red, to the slider to convey the time duration portion when playing the video.

We also added a tooltip on the slide of the timeline to show the time duration of the video.

Missing Feedback:

  • Tooltip

“but there is a peak firmly of fear: 26% of disgust, 25% of anger, 10% of fear, can I see what happened at that moment of the interaction” -- Tutor1

This early version of the tooltip was not helpful, because the user was placing the cursor around a peak and reading information from the tooltip at the bottom of the page. The information was too far from user’s attention.

We changed this by adding a tooltip to the streamgraph directly showing percentage of emotions when hovered.

Missing contextual information:

  • shared documents
  • shared chats
  • video coach

“To contextualize, the learner has approved a negative emotion, but if I do not know why, what provoked her, there is no remediation possible .. if I can not correlate that with a shared document whether the document did not please her, that she did not understand, it was not clear, or not a suitable theme etc, [..] I would like to click on the document to see” -- Tutor1

“What is it said in chat, what is it said in chat, I would like to have access to chat” -- Tutor1

“It is [a positive peak] clearly a reaction to what the tutor tells her [..] but as we do not have his feedback it's frustrating [meaning tutor’s voice]” -- Tutor1

In this early version, the timeline shows only contextual interactions but not their content.

We changed this by adding the possibility to display shared documents in another tab of the browser, and chart messages in a tooltip.

We also added video record of the pair tutor-learner side-by-side.

History view

“This [History view] shows an overview for each learning session, however if we have 30 sessions, we will be stuck” -- Tutor1

We added the brush view to allow navigation through multiple sessions.

Bug / Spelling errors

We identified and corrected other bugs:

- a bug with the position of the cursor it changes when using a mouse

- a bug with the data displayed in the tooltip next to the video, the emotions relate to a fixed time segment, sometimes we end up with information that do not belong to the position of the cursor.

- some legends, for instance, where not spelled well.

We addressed the raised usability issues. And we followed with a second test.

Test with tutor 2 : 55 minutes

Invisibility:

  •  Timeline’s Slider

“I find [..] I do not know how to call it [slider] was a bit hidden” -- Tutor2

Although we added two colors to slider of the timeline: grey and red to convey time duration of the video, the user highlighted that the slider was hidden.

We decided to adopt a black background for this view so that all the markers will be more visible.

Navigation

  • Video navigation through peaks

“It interesting because I clicked on the biggest peak of happiness in the timeline, and I arrive at a moment when I see that there is actually a smile” -- Tutor2

“13:40 [Tutor read the duration from the tooltip of the player], there is a big purple peak that must correspond to a peak of fear and apparently yes there is a moment of embarrassment where it happens she can not express herself. For a tutor this is very useful in fact, especially the peaks at first I would go see the peaks directly, to see what it is like. As a tutor it is really useful to go directly to a place and find a moment of the session like that. After some peaks are too much marked [meaning small peaks in streamgraph] you have to navigate.” -- Tutor2

The user was able to navigate through the video easily  using peaks, which was a change resulting from the first usability test: enabling navigation using streamgraph.

The video was a little bit far from user attention. Interacting with emotions and watching the recording was not optimal.

We decided to place the video above the timeline, so to mimic a real player.

Tooltip Feedback

  • Slider tooltip

“I see at 27:40 that there is a little purple thing that goes down and it corresponds to the fear in any case it corresponds to the moment of stress that I'm viewing” -- Tutor2

Throughout the session the user used the tooltip of the slider to check moment of peaks. This also was a change resulting from the first usability test.

Contextual information:

  • shared documents
  • shared chats

“The process of displaying the chat and the documents is interesting” -- Tutor2

The user highlights the importance of enabling access to the content, which was a change resulting from the first usability test.
